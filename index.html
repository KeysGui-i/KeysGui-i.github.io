STEP BY STEP

How to start your first game

STEP 1
Getting started

First, tap your arcade card or insert coins/credits, depending on your arcade. If you do not have an aime card yet, you can start in guest mode. If you do have an aime card, tap it first to log into your account.

One maimai machine can support two players at once. If you want to play with a friend, be prepared to pay for two players. Also, if one side of the machine is already in use, the other side cannot be joined until that game ends.

STEP 2
Follow the beginner guide.

Simply follow the beginner tutorial all the way through. It will introduce the basic game mechanics and help you understand how the game works. It is highly recommended not to skip it, especially if this is your first time playing, because it is very useful!

STEP 3
Select a song.

Choose the song you want to play. Each song usually has 4 to 5 difficulty levels. If you are a beginner, it is best to start with lower levels, such as level 1 to level 5, so you can get used to the rhythm, note patterns, and button positions first.

STEP 4
Hit the buttons with the rhythm.

Watch the notes and press the eight buttons around the screen in time with the music. At first, just try to stay on beat and finish the song.

FAQ

Things beginners often worry about

Do I need gloves and earphones to play maimai?
If you just want to try maimai for the first time, they are not required. But if you end up enjoying the game and want to play more often, bringing gloves and wired earphones is recommended for a better overall experience.
How do I log into an account?
To log into a SEGA rhythm game account, you usually need a physical Aime card. Many Round1 locations sell these cards. In many cases, a new card also comes with one free play.
The notes feel too slow and hard to read. I see other players using much faster note speed. How do I change that?
After choosing your song and difficulty color (green, yellow, red, purple, or white), press the right-side button and then confirm to enter the settings menu. If you want the notes to move faster, a good beginner starting point is around 4.5 note speed.
One side of the machine looks empty, so why does nothing happen when I insert coins?
That is because both sides of one maimai machine are linked during gameplay. If someone is already playing on one side, the other side usually cannot start a separate game at the same time. It is best to wait until that player finishes before inserting your coins, since doing it in the middle of someone else’s game may interrupt them.
There are already a lot of people playing maimai. Do I have to wait until everyone is completely done before it is my turn?
Usually no. maimai is commonly played in rotation. After one person or one pair finishes a round (usually 3 to 4 songs), they should let the next person in line play. So if you want a turn, you can wait nearby and politely let the current players know that you would like to play next.
Everyone playing maimai looks really good. I have never played before, so I feel a little intimidated.
Every strong player started as a beginner too. Most maimai players will not laugh at you for being new. In fact, many of them are happy to explain the game or help you adjust your settings, especially because there are still not that many people who know the game well.
